About

Hi, I’m George. I was born and raised in Palo Alto, CA, and I’m currently a senior at Tufts University, where I study cognitive science and computer science. I'm passionate about learning and building products that are simple and useful. Additionally, I conduct research focused on how humans empathize with and obey robots in the Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) lab at Tufts.

Here in Boston, I’m also a writing tutor and I enjoy playing club baseball in the NECBA. I've worked at my fair share of startups over the past few summers, and in high school, I spent lots of time working on a sports magazine with friends. Some of my current interests include cognitive science, product design and management, HRI, brain-computer interfaces, computer science, and law. I’m also fond of baseball, backpacking, traveling (especially in Latin America – I worked in Costa Rica this summer), and rooting for the San Francisco Giants.

As a college senior, one of my favorite things to do is meet new, interesting people and learn from them. If you’d like to get in touch, please don’t hesistate to contact me.
