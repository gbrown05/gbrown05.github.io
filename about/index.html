About

I was born and raised in Palo Alto, CA, and I work as a Business Intelligence and Analytics Consultant at Thorogood Associates in Philadelphia. I recently graduated from Tufts University, where I studied cognitive science and computer science. I'm passionate about learning and reading, and I especially enjoy thinking about how individuals and society react to new technology. To this end, I conducted research focused on how humans empathize with and obey robots in the Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) lab at Tufts.

In Boston, I was also a writing tutor and played club baseball in the NECBA. I've worked at several startups, and in high school I spent lots of time working on the first high school sports magazine in the country with friends. Some of my current interests include law, cognitive science, HRI, brain-computer interfaces, computer science, and philosophy. I’m also fond of baseball, backpacking, traveling (especially in Latin America – I worked in Costa Rica for several months in 2014), and rooting for the San Francisco Giants.

If you’d like to get in touch, please don’t hesistate to contact me.
